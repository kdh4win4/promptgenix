Research Strategy

Technical Approach

Phase I focuses on building a deterministic foundation. We move beyond "prompting" by treating LLMs as constrained narrative layers sitting atop a rigorous Bayesian decision engine.

Innovation & Rigor

  • Evidence-weighted QC: Standardized QC gates automatically discount low-quality or high-noise datasets.
  • Strict Claim Gating: Our "No evidence → No claim" architecture ensures the LLM cannot author confidence; it only interprets deterministic engine outputs.
  • Conflict Detection: Explicitly flags "conflicting evidence" to prevent confirmation bias in hypothesis ranking.
  • Deterministic Runs: Pinned software versions and checksums ensure results are 100% reproducible.

Risks & Mitigation

Risk: LLM attempts to hallucinate connections not present in the data.

Mitigation: Implementation of a Reference-Integrity Layer that cross-checks LLM citations against a deterministic database of evidence objects before final assembly.

Risk: Insufficient public data for certain hypotheses.

Mitigation: Fallback mode that outputs "Candidate hypotheses" with low-confidence tags and specific data-needed guidance.

Phase I Success Criteria

Reviewer-friendly principle: Every claim must be traceable to (1) evidence objects derived from public data or (2) cited literature, with uncertainty explicitly stated via credible intervals.

Success in Phase I is defined by demonstrable traceability, calibrated uncertainty, and usefulness for guiding study design decisions, ensuring a solid de-risking path for Phase II enterprise scaling.
