Team strategy

Separate “confidence” from “explanation” by design.

PromptGenix is engineered to maintain reviewer trust by clearly separating (i) quantitative confidence from (ii) narrative interpretation. Confidence is computed by a deterministic, evidence-weighted Bayesian decision engine, while the LLM is constrained to explain rankings and cite supporting or conflicting sources.

Reviewer-facing claim: The LLM does not determine confidence; it explains confidence computed by the Bayesian engine, and every statement must be supported by traceable evidence links and/or citations.

Bayesian engine responsibility boundary

The team implements a clear responsibility split that maps to the system architecture:

  • Bayesian engine: computes posterior confidence and credible intervals from standardized evidence features.
  • Evidence layer: generates effect sizes, uncertainty, and quality weights from public datasets.
  • LLM layer: produces human-readable rationale constrained to evidence objects (no evidence → no claim).
For each hypothesis H and evidence set E = {e1, e2, ..., en}:

P(H | E)  ∝  P(H) · Π_i P(e_i | H)

Posterior summary reported as:
  - posterior mean/median confidence
  - 95% credible interval (CrI)
  - evidence coverage: supporting vs. conflicting vs. missing
Why this matters: This separation improves auditability, reduces “black-box” risk, and aligns the team’s skills with a transparent, evidence-weighted decision workflow suitable for SBIR review.
Phase I operating model

Fast iteration, strict traceability, measurable utility.

Delivery cadence

  • Weekly build cycles: evidence ingestion → posterior scoring → report generation → QA checks.
  • Traceability checklist: every ranked hypothesis links to evidence objects + supporting/conflicting citations.
  • Reproducibility gates: pinned versions, config snapshots, and checksums verified on reruns.

Phase I success indicators

  • Deterministic reruns: consistent outputs for the same inputs/configs.
  • Transparent uncertainty: posterior + CrI + coverage included in outputs.
  • Usability: pilot feedback indicates the ranked hypotheses support at least one concrete next-step decision.
Commitment to responsible use: PromptGenix is designed to support—not replace—human scientific decision-making, with explicit uncertainty labeling and evidence-first reasoning.
