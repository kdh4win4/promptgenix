SIGNIFICANCE

Immunology and infectious disease research programs routinely generate large volumes of RNA-Seq, single-cell RNA-Seq, and high-dimensional flow cytometry data. Despite major advances in data generation, biological insight remains a bottleneck. Analysis workflows are fragmented across tools, rely heavily on custom scripts, and require scarce bioinformatics expertise. As a result, turnaround times are long, reproducibility is inconsistent, and interpretation often depends on manual literature review by overextended investigators.

This gap directly limits the speed and quality of hypothesis generation, experimental design, and decision-making in NIH-funded studies, particularly in resource-constrained academic and government laboratories.

INNOVATION

PromptGenix introduces an integrated BioAI co-investigator that combines end-to-end automated omics pipelines with a literature-aware internal LLM Hypothesis Generator.

Unlike existing platforms that stop at statistical results or static summaries, PromptGenix produces ranked, testable biological hypotheses and concrete follow-up experiment suggestions grounded in traceable PubMed-aware reasoning.

OVERALL OBJECTIVE & CENTRAL HYPOTHESIS

Objective: Deliver a validated Phase I prototype that automates RNA-Seq and flow cytometry workflows and generates reviewer-ready reports with ranked biological hypotheses and suggested validation experiments.

Central hypothesis: Standardized, reproducible pipelines combined with traceable, literature-aware LLM reasoning will reduce analysis turnaround time and increase interpretability, enabling faster and higher-quality study decisions in immunology and infectious disease research.

SPECIFIC AIMS

  • Aim 1. Harden end-to-end automated RNA-Seq and flow cytometry pipelines using public datasets (GEO, SRA, FlowRepository).
  • Aim 2. Implement a traceable Hypothesis Generator that ranks biological hypotheses and proposes validation experiments using PubMed-aware reasoning.
  • Aim 3. Generate exportable HTML/PDF reports and evaluate usability through 2–3 pilot studies using public or de-identified datasets.

EXPECTED OUTCOMES & DELIVERABLES

  • Validated prototype producing reproducible, versioned outputs across reruns
  • Reviewer-ready reports generated within <24 hours from raw data inputs
  • Measured reductions in turnaround time and positive user feedback on AI-generated hypotheses

IMPACT

Successful completion of Phase I will establish PromptGenix as a scalable BioAI co-investigator platform for immunology and infectious disease research. Phase I validation will rely exclusively on public datasets to avoid IP or data-use conflicts and will position the platform for Phase II expansion into multi-site studies and secure deployments.
