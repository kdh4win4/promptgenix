Specific Aims (Phase I): PromptGenix – BioAI Automation Platform

PromptGenix LLC (Women & Minority-Owned) • Contact: dohoon.kim1@icloud.com • Website: promptgenix.org/sbir.html

Significance

Immunology and infectious disease programs generate large volumes of RNA-Seq, scRNA-Seq, and high-dimensional flow cytometry data, yet analysis often depends on fragmented tools, custom scripts, and limited expert time. This drives long turnaround times, inconsistent reproducibility, and delayed biological interpretation.

Innovation

PromptGenix integrates end-to-end automated pipelines with an internal, literature-aware LLM layer that produces interpretable summaries and a Hypothesis Generator that ranks mechanistic hypotheses and proposes concrete validation experiments, grounded in PubMed-aware reasoning.

Overall Objective & Central Hypothesis

Objective: Deliver a validated, reproducible Phase I prototype that automates RNA-Seq and flow cytometry workflows and generates reviewer-ready reports with ranked hypotheses and follow-up experiments.

Central hypothesis: Standardized automation combined with traceable, literature-aware LLM reasoning will reduce analysis turnaround time and increase interpretability, enabling faster, higher-quality study decisions in infectious disease and vaccine research.

Specific Aims

  • Aim 1: Harden end-to-end automated RNA-Seq and flow cytometry pipelines using public datasets (GEO/SRA/FlowRepository).
  • Aim 2: Implement a traceable Hypothesis Generator that ranks hypotheses and proposes validation experiments with PubMed-aware citations.
  • Aim 3: Generate exportable HTML/PDF reports and evaluate usability via 2–3 pilot partners using public or de-identified datasets.

Expected Outcomes & Deliverables

  • Validated prototype producing reproducible outputs across reruns (versioned configs + reports).
  • Reviewer-ready reports generated within <24 hours from raw data inputs.
  • Measured improvements in turnaround time and user-rated usefulness of AI hypotheses.

Impact

Successful completion of Phase I will establish PromptGenix as a scalable BioAI co-investigator platform for immunology studies, enabling Phase II expansion to multi-site cohorts, secure deployments, and broader multi-omics integration.

Note: Phase I validation will use public datasets to avoid IP/data-use conflicts. Commercialization targets NIH-funded labs, government research groups, and CROs.
