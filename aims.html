Specific Aims (Phase I): PromptGenix – Hypothesis Intelligence Engine for Public Biomedical Data

PromptGenix LLC (Women & Minority-Owned) · Contact: dohoon.kim1@icloud.com · Website: promptgenix.org/sbir.html

Significance

Public biomedical resources now contain vast volumes of omics and immune profiling datasets and a rapidly expanding scientific literature, yet researchers still face a critical bottleneck: turning heterogeneous evidence into prioritized, testable hypotheses. Most workflows rely on fragmented tools, bespoke scripts, and scarce expert time, leading to long turnaround, inconsistent reproducibility, and delayed experimental decisions.

The key gap is not only computation, but evidence-weighted scientific synthesis—specifically, the lack of scalable and transparent mechanisms to integrate public datasets and literature into ranked hypotheses with quantified uncertainty.

Innovation

PromptGenix introduces a hypothesis intelligence engine that prioritizes mechanistic hypotheses using statistical evidence modeling and Bayesian updating over public biomedical data and literature-derived priors. Large language models are used strictly for interpretability—to explain rankings and cite supporting or conflicting sources— not to determine confidence.

Unlike tools that primarily produce analysis outputs or narrative summaries, PromptGenix delivers evidence-linked hypothesis rankings with explicit uncertainty labeling and actionable next-step recommendations.

Overall objective & central hypothesis

Objective: Deliver a validated Phase I prototype that automatically retrieves public datasets and literature, constructs standardized evidence objects, and outputs ranked biological hypotheses with probabilistic confidence, traceable evidence links, and recommended follow-up experiments.

Central hypothesis: An evidence-weighted decision engine that combines statistical scoring and Bayesian inference with constrained LLM interpretability will reduce hypothesis generation time while improving transparency, reproducibility, and usefulness for guiding study design decisions in immunology and infectious disease research.

Specific aims

  • Aim 1. Build a robust public-data ingest and evidence feature layer that generates standardized evidence objects (effect sizes, uncertainty, reproducibility, and context descriptors) from selected public datasets and associated metadata.
  • Aim 2. Implement an evidence-weighted hypothesis prioritization engine using statistical scoring and Bayesian updating to compute posterior confidence distributions and rank candidate hypotheses with explicit uncertainty annotations.
  • Aim 3. Deliver reviewer-ready HTML/PDF outputs and pilot usability evaluation demonstrating traceability and utility: ranked hypotheses with confidence bands, evidence coverage (supporting vs. conflicting), citations, and recommended next steps.

Expected outcomes & deliverables

  • A validated prototype that produces deterministic, reproducible evidence objects and ranked hypotheses across reruns (versioned configs, checksums, and traceable artifacts).
  • Evidence-weighted hypothesis outputs with posterior confidence estimates and clear uncertainty labeling, including links to supporting and conflicting evidence.
  • Reviewer-ready reports generated within <24 hours for accession-driven runs (dataset-dependent), with reduced manual synthesis time in pilot feedback.

Impact

Successful completion of Phase I will de-risk Phase II development of a scalable hypothesis intelligence platform that enables faster, more transparent evidence integration and prioritization for experimental planning—supporting, not replacing, human scientific decision-making.
