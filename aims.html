PromptGenix
NIH SBIR Phase I – Specific Aims

Specific Aims

Modern drug discovery is hindered not by a lack of data, but by the unreliability of automated synthesis. PromptGenix addresses this by building an evidence-weighted hypothesis intelligence engine that replaces black-box AI outputs with traceable, Bayesian-ranked insights. Our Phase I goal is to demonstrate a deterministic pipeline that mitigates LLM hallucinations and accelerates rigorous study design.

Hypothesis

A deterministic Bayesian scoring engine, decoupled from generative narrative layers, can produce reproducible hypothesis rankings that significantly improve the transparency and speed of evidence-based experimental planning.

Specific Aims
  • Aim 1: Automate data-to-evidence telemetry pipelines using selected public datasets, producing reproducible and versioned evidence objects with standardized QC gates.
  • Aim 2: Implement an evidence-weighted hypothesis prioritization engine using statistical scoring and Bayesian updating to compute posterior confidence and rank candidate hypotheses.
  • Aim 3: Generate reviewer-ready HTML/PDF reports and perform pilot evaluation demonstrating quantifiable traceability and utility ratings (>80%) for guiding concrete experimental decisions.
Expected outcomes & deliverables
  • A validated prototype producing reproducible evidence objects and ranked hypotheses with posterior confidence estimates.
  • Reviewer-ready reports generated within <24 hours for accession-driven runs, ensuring rapid iteration for research teams.
Impact

Phase I will de-risk the development of a scalable hypothesis intelligence platform that enables faster, more transparent evidence integration for experimental planning, supporting—but not replacing—human scientific decision-making.
