How labs and trials can actually use PromptGenix.

Below are concrete scenarios showing how PromptGenix automates RNA-Seq and flow cytometry analysis and produces interpretable reports for translational research, vaccine studies, and multi-site cohorts.

Use Case 1 · Flow Cytometry Automation for Immune Profiling

Flow cytometry · COMPASS

Question

“In this vaccine or HIV study, which T-cell subsets become more polyfunctional after stimulation, and how consistent is this across participants and sites?”

Current pain points

  • Manual gating in FlowJo across thousands of FCS files.
  • Site-to-site batch effects make thresholds non-reproducible.
  • COMPASS analysis requires custom R scripts and manual QC.

PromptGenix solution

  • Automated preprocessing (compensation, transformation, QC) of FCS files.
  • Template-based or ML-assisted gating and batch normalization.
  • Push-button COMPASS workflows with standardized outputs.
  • LLM-generated summary: “CD8+ polyfunctional responses increased in Arm B, especially IFN-γ/TNF-α producers.”

Use Case 2 · RNA-Seq & scRNA-Seq for Mechanism Discovery

Bulk RNA-Seq · scRNA-Seq

Question

“Which pathways and cell types change between responders vs. non-responders, or between CD16+ and CD16− γδ T-cell subsets?”

Current pain points

  • Fragmented pipelines (STAR/RSEM/DESeq2/edgeR/GSEA) that are hard to reuse.
  • scRNA-Seq analysis (Seurat/scanpy) requires specialized expertise.
  • Results reported as long gene lists that are hard to interpret biologically.

PromptGenix solution

  • Standardized pipelines from raw FASTQ → QC → alignment → quantification → DEG → GSEA/GSVA.
  • Optional scRNA-Seq workflows (Seurat/scanpy) with cell-type annotation.
  • Automated exports of DEG tables, volcano plots, and pathway summaries.
  • LLM-assisted narrative: “Type I IFN and cytotoxicity signatures are enriched in CD16+ γδ T cells।”

Use Case 3 · Integrated NGS + Cytometry for Clinical Trials

Multi-modal · Trial-ready

Question

“Can we generate a unified immunology report per visit/patient that combines RNA-Seq, scRNA-Seq, and flow cytometry results in a format clinicians can read?”

Current pain points

  • Each assay analyzed separately, reported in different formats and timelines.
  • No single place that integrates multi-omics signals for the PI or DSMB.
  • Reproducibility and auditability are difficult across protocol amendments.

PromptGenix solution

  • Unified workflow that ingests FASTQ, FCS, and metadata per subject/visit.
  • Generates integrated HTML/PDF reports with key signatures, cell subsets, and safety/efficacy markers.
  • Standardized templates that can be reused across protocols and sites.
  • Phase II vision: user management, audit trails, and deployment in secure VPCs or hospital environments.
