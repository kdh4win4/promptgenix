PromptGenix – Specific Aims (Phase I)

Hypothesis Intelligence Engine for Public Biomedical Data · SBIR (NIH/NIAID focus)
Public-data validation Bayesian evidence updating Reproducibility + traceability LLM for interpretability only

Goal: Faster, transparent, evidence-weighted hypothesis prioritization

Phase I deliverable: a validated prototype that outputs ranked hypotheses with quantified uncertainty and traceable evidence links

PromptGenix automates retrieval of public omics datasets and scientific literature, converts them into standardized evidence objects, and produces ranked, testable biological hypotheses with explicit uncertainty and actionable next steps.

Significance

Public biomedical repositories contain vast volumes of omics and immune profiling datasets, yet researchers still face a critical bottleneck: converting heterogeneous evidence into prioritized, testable hypotheses. Current workflows rely on fragmented tools, ad hoc scripts, and expert-driven synthesis, resulting in slow turnaround, poor reproducibility, and opaque decision-making. What is missing is a scalable, transparent mechanism to integrate public datasets and literature into ranked hypotheses with quantified uncertainty.

Innovation

PromptGenix introduces a hypothesis intelligence engine that prioritizes hypotheses using statistical evidence modeling and Bayesian updating over public biomedical data and literature-derived priors. Large language models are used only for interpretability—to explain rankings and cite supporting or conflicting evidence—never to assign confidence. The system delivers evidence-linked rankings with explicit uncertainty and recommended next experiments.

Overall Objective & Central Hypothesis

Objective: Develop and validate a Phase I prototype that automatically retrieves public datasets and literature, constructs standardized evidence objects, and outputs ranked biological hypotheses with probabilistic confidence, traceable evidence links, and recommended follow-up experiments.

Central Hypothesis: An evidence-weighted decision engine combining statistical scoring, Bayesian inference, and constrained LLM-based interpretation will reduce hypothesis generation time while improving transparency, reproducibility, and utility for experimental study design in immunology and infectious disease research.

Specific Aims

Aim 1 Construct a standardized public-data evidence layer

Develop automated pipelines to ingest selected public omics datasets and associated metadata and generate deterministic evidence objects capturing effect size, uncertainty, reproducibility indicators, and biological context.

Aim 2 Implement an evidence-weighted hypothesis prioritization engine

Apply statistical scoring and Bayesian updating to integrate multiple evidence objects, compute posterior confidence distributions, and rank candidate biological hypotheses with explicit uncertainty annotations.

Aim 3 Demonstrate traceable, reviewer-ready outputs and usability

Generate HTML/PDF reports that present ranked hypotheses with confidence bands, supporting versus conflicting evidence coverage, citations, and recommended next experiments, and conduct a pilot usability evaluation focused on interpretability and decision support.

Expected outcomes: deterministic, reproducible hypothesis rankings across reruns; quantified uncertainty; transparent evidence traceability; reviewer-ready reports generated within <24 hours for accession-driven runs (dataset-dependent).
