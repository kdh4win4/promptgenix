The problem

Terabytes of omics data, but insights arrive too late.

Modern immunology and genomics programs collect huge volumes of RNA-Seq, scRNA-Seq, and flow cytometry data, but analysis often depends on fragmented tools, custom scripts, and a handful of over-extended experts. Turnaround is measured in weeks, not hours, and every project feels like a one-off.

  • Multiple disconnected tools (STAR, RSEM, Seurat, FlowJo, COMPASS, GSEA)
  • Siloed code that is hard to reuse and harder to maintain
  • Limited access to biostatistics and bioinformatics expertise
  • Scientific interpretation left to whoever has time to read the literature
PromptGenix solution

An end-to-end BioAI co-pilot for analysis, interpretation, and hypothesis.

PromptGenix automates QC, alignment, DEG, GSEA/GSVA, and flow cytometry gating in reproducible pipelines. On top of that, our internal LLM layer reads the results, cross-references the literature, and proposes mechanistic hypotheses and validation experiments that can be dropped into a grant or lab meeting slide.

  • One-click pipelines for RNA-Seq, scRNA-Seq, and flow cytometry
  • Exportable HTML/PDF reports with plain-language summaries
  • Configurable to run entirely on-prem (Mac Studio) or in a secure AWS VPC
SBIR focus: Phase I will harden the automated pipelines and hypothesis generator on public datasets, then apply them to partner HIV/vaccine cohorts to demonstrate impact on study design.
