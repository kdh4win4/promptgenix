Commercialization Summary

PromptGenix is a BioAI automation platform that turns RNA-Seq and flow cytometry inputs into reproducible pipelines, reviewer-ready reports, and a PubMed-aware Hypothesis Generator that proposes testable hypotheses and concrete validation experiments. Commercialization targets NIH-funded labs, government research groups, and CROs that need faster, reproducible, interpretation-driven analysis.

Contact: dohoon.kim1@icloud.com · Women & Minority-Owned small business positioning
Target customers

Who pays and why

  • NIH-funded academic labs (immunology, infectious disease, vaccines)
  • Government research groups needing on-prem, controlled workflows
  • CROs / translational teams seeking faster turnaround and standardized reporting
Primary buyer: PI / lab director (budget owner).
Primary user: analyst, bioinformatician, or senior trainee.
Pain points

What breaks today

  • Tool fragmentation (alignment/QC, flow gating, enrichment, reporting)
  • Manual scripting and inconsistent reproducibility
  • Weeks of turnaround and difficult onboarding of new analysts
  • Interpretation burden (literature reading, mechanistic framing)
Result: slow study iteration, delayed hypothesis formation, and inconsistent outputs.
Value proposition

Automation + interpretation + hypothesis (not just plots)

  • Reproducible pipelines: versioned configs + deterministic outputs + standardized reports
  • AI co-investigator: structured summaries and ranked hypotheses with PubMed-linked evidence
  • Deployment options: on-prem (local) or secure AWS VPC for compliant workflows
Phase I establishes technical feasibility and trust through public-data validation and traceable reporting.
Product

What is sold

PromptGenix is delivered as a packaged workflow + reporting system that can run on a local workstation or in a cloud environment. The platform produces exportable HTML/PDF reports with methods, figures, key findings, and a structured hypothesis section.

RNA-Seq pipelines Flow cytometry workflows Reports (HTML/PDF) Hypothesis Generator
Differentiation

Why PromptGenix wins

  • End-to-end: integrates analysis + reporting + interpretation
  • Traceability: citations and evidence linkage for scientific claims
  • Actionability: experiment suggestions aligned to real lab constraints
  • Security posture: on-prem execution option for sensitive programs
SBIR fit: Phase I reduces technical risk and accelerates Phase II pilots and adoption.
Business model

SaaS + Enterprise options

  • Annual subscription per lab / team
  • Optional compute add-on (cloud)
  • Professional services for onboarding
Pricing (initial)

Simple and scalable

  • Starter: small lab, on-prem workflows
  • Pro: multi-project + reporting templates
  • Enterprise: VPC / compliance + support
Exact pricing will be refined during Phase I customer discovery.
Early revenue

Phase II readiness

  • Pilot conversions (subscription)
  • Institutional site licenses
  • CRO workflow packaging
Go-to-market

Phase I customer discovery + Phase II pilots

Phase I will conduct structured customer discovery with PIs, analysts, and core facilities to refine product requirements, deployment expectations, and pricing. Phase II will focus on expanded pilots and adoption in real study pipelines.

Phase I (now)
  • 10–20 discovery interviews (PI/analyst/core)
  • 2–3 pilot partners for usability scoring (public/de-identified)
  • Refine “Hypothesis usefulness” rubric and reporting formats
Phase II (next)
  • Expanded pilots (multi-site cohorts)
  • Production-grade deployment options (on-prem / VPC)
  • Commercial subscriptions and institutional licensing
Channel strategy: NIH study networks, PI referrals, core facilities, and government research collaborations.
Milestones

Commercial readiness checkpoints

  • M1: Phase I prototype validated on ≥3 public datasets (reproducibility + speed)
  • M2: Hypothesis Generator produces traceable, actionable hypotheses with citations
  • M3: Pilot partners confirm usability and value (structured rubric)
  • M4: Phase II pilot plan + pricing model refined from discovery interviews
